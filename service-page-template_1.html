Signs you may need tile roof work

Common roof clues are easier to evaluate when the inspection is tied to photos you can review.

Broken or missing tiles

Open areas that can expose the roof system below.

Underlayment wear

Aging material beneath the tile that often drives the real scope.

Valley or penetration leak

Water entry around high-flow areas or roof openings.

Slipped rows

Tile movement that changes the water path and appearance.

Storm movement

Post-monsoon shifts, impact marks, or loose material.

Finished-surface concerns

Visible tile damage that needs a clean repair plan.

What we document

The scope should connect to real roof conditions, not vague sales language.

  • Tile condition, staged areas, and vulnerable rows
  • Underlayment clues and battens where visible
  • Valleys, penetrations, flashing, and transitions
  • A written scope with clear repair or replacement options

Tile roofing FAQ

Do you repair tile roofs or only replace them?
We handle both. We document the roof condition and recommend a targeted repair when it fits, or a larger scope when the roof system calls for it.

How do I know if it's the tile or the underlayment?
Tile often outlasts the underlayment beneath it. Our inspection identifies which layer is failing so the scope matches the real problem.
